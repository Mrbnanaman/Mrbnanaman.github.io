history of cheese

Cheese making began accidentally around 8000 BCE with the domestication of animals, likely when milk stored in animal stomach pouches (containing natural rennet) curdled, creating curds and whey; it became a key portable food, spread by ancient civilizations like the Romans, and was refined by monks during the Middle Ages, leading to the vast variety we know today, with early versions being salty and crumbly like feta, notes Wikipedia and The Spruce Eats
